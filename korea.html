About Korea

South Korea is a country in East Asia known for its rich history, advanced technology, and vibrant culture. Cities like Seoul and Busan offer modern skyscrapers, shopping districts, and nightlife, while traditional palaces, temples, and hanok villages showcase Korea’s heritage. Korea is famous for its K-pop, traditional festivals, and unique cuisine. The country has four distinct seasons, allowing tourists to enjoy cherry blossoms in spring, beautiful mountains in autumn, and snowy landscapes in winter.



Foods


  • Kimchi

    Kimchi is a traditional Korean fermented vegetable dish, usually made with napa cabbage and Korean radish, seasoned with chili powder, garlic, ginger, and salted seafood. It is a staple in Korean cuisine.


    Recipe

    • 1 medium napa cabbage
    • 1/4 cup sea salt
    • 4 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 3 tablespoons Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)

    Instructions: Cut cabbage, soak in salted water, rinse, prepare seasoning paste

  • Bibimbap

Tourist Destination


1. Gyeongbokgung Palace, Seoul





Gyeongbokgung Palace is a grand royal palace in Seoul, built in 1395. It features traditional Korean architecture, beautiful gardens, and hosts the changing of the guard ceremony, offering a glimpse into Korea’s royal history.


2. Jeju Island





Jeju Island is a volcanic island known for its natural beauty, waterfalls, lava tubes, and beaches. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and those seeking a relaxing getaway.

Languages


The official language of South Korea is Korean (Hangul). It is a phonetic language with a unique script invented in the 15th century.
