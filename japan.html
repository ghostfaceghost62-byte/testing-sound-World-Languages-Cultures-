About Japan

Japan is famous for its Cherry Blossoms, temples, mountains, and rich culture. Japan is a country where old traditions and modern life exist side by side, making it a fascinating destination for tourists. You can walk through quiet temples and shrines in the morning, then see bright neon lights and busy streets at night. Cities like Tokyo and Osaka offer shopping, technology, and food from all over the world, while places like Kyoto and Nara are known for their historical sites and traditional culture. Tourists often enjoy Japan's seasonal beauty. In spring, cherry blossoms fill parks and streets, creating a peaceful atmosphere. Autumn is also popular because of the colorful leaves in the mountains and gardens. Each season offers different festivals, foods, and activities to experience. Japanese food is another highlight for visitors. Sushi, ramen, tempura, and street food are easy to find and come in many styles and flavors. Even simple meals are usually prepared with care and attention to detail. Transportation in Japan is very convenient, especially the trains, which are clean, fast, and easy to use. Most people are polite and helpful, even if they don't speak much English. Overall, Japan offers a safe, clean, and unforgettable travel experience for tourists.



Foods


  • Sushi

    Sushi is made of vinegared rice combined with raw or cooked fish, seafood, and sometimes vegetables. It originally began as a method of preserving fish using fermented rice in ancient Japan. Today, sushi is a popular dish worldwide, served in restaurants, homes, and convenience stores.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 2 cups sushi rice
    - 2 1/2 cups water
    - 1/3 cup rice vinegar
    - 3 tablespoons sugar
    - 1 teaspoon salt
    - Nori (seaweed sheets)
    - Fresh fish (like tuna, salmon) or vegetables (like cucumber, avocado)
    - Soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger for serving

    Instructions:
    1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    2. Combine the rice and water in a rice cooker and cook according to the manufacturer's instructions.
    3. In a small saucepan, heat the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved. Let it cool.
    4. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Let it cool to room temperature.
    5. Place a nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving about an inch at the top edge.
    6. Add your choice of fish or vegetables in a line across the center of the rice.
    7. Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure.
    8. Slice the roll into bite-sized pieces using a sharp knife.
    9. Serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.


  • Ramen

    Ramen is made of wheat noodles served in a flavour broth with toppings such as pork, eggs, and vegetables. It was introduced from China in the late 19th century and became a popular everyday meal in Japan. Today, it is a favourite comfort food with many regional styles across Japan.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
    - 2 packs of ramen noodles
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon miso paste (optional)
    - 1 teaspoon sesame oil
    - 2 boiled eggs, halved
    - Sliced green onions
    - Sliced cooked pork or chicken (optional)
    - Corn, bamboo shoots, or other vegetables (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. In a pot, bring the broth to a boil. Add soy sauce, miso paste (if using), and sesame oil. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    2. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
    3. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls.
    4. Pour the hot broth over the noodles.
    5. Top with boiled egg halves, sliced green onions, cooked pork or chicken, and any additional vegetables you like.
    6. Serve hot and enjoy!


  • Tempura

    Tempura is made of seafood or vegetables coated in a light batter and deep-fried. It was introduced to Japan by Portuguese traders in the 16th century. The dish was adapted to Japanese tastes and cooking methods. Today, tempura is commonly served in restaurants, bento boxes, and traditional meals.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cup all-purpose flour
    - 1 cup ice-cold water
    - 1 egg
    - Assorted vegetables (like sweet potato, bell pepper, zucchini)
    - Seafood (like shrimp, squid)
    - Vegetable oil for frying
    - Dipping sauce (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. In a bowl, lightly beat the egg. Add ice-cold water and mix.
    2. Add the flour to the egg mixture and gently mix until just combined. Do not overmix; it's okay if there are some lumps.
    3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
    4. Dip the vegetables and seafood into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off.
    5. Carefully place the battered items into the hot oil and fry until golden and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
    6. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
    7. Serve hot with dipping sauce if desired.


  • Takoyaki

    Takoyaki is made of wheat batter filled with pieces of octopus and cooked into round balls. It was created in Osaka in the 1930s as an affordable street food. The snack quickly became popular among locals. Today, takoyaki is a famous street food sold at festivals and food stalls throughout Japan.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cup all-purpose flour
    - 2 eggs
    - 2 cups dashi stock (or water)
    - 1/2 cup chopped cooked octopus
    - 1/4 cup chopped green onions
    - 1/4 cup pickled ginger, chopped
    - Takoyaki sauce
    - Mayonnaise
    - Bonito flakes (optional)
    - Aonori (seaweed flakes, optional)

    Instructions:
    1. In a bowl, mix the flour, eggs, and dashi stock until smooth.
    2. Preheat a takoyaki pan and lightly oil each compartment.
    3. Pour the batter into each compartment, filling them about 3/4 full.
    4. Add a piece of octopus, some green onions, and pickled ginger to each compartment.
    5. Once the edges start to cook, use a skewer to turn the takoyaki balls 90 degrees. Continue turning them until they are evenly golden brown and cooked through.
    6. Remove from the pan and drizzle with takoyaki sauce and mayonnaise.
    7. Sprinkle with bonito flakes and aonori if desired. Serve hot.


  • Udon

    Udon is made of thick noodles served in a simple broth. It has been eaten in Japan since ancient times. Different regions developed their own styles of udon. Today, udon is enjoyed hot in winter and cold in summer.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 200g dried udon noodles
    - 4 cups dashi stock
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon mirin
    - Sliced green onions
    - Tempura flakes or fish cake (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. Cook the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
    2. In a pot, bring the dashi stock to a boil. Add soy sauce and mirin. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    3. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls.
    4. Pour the hot broth over the noodles.
    5. Top with sliced green onions and tempura flakes or fish cake if desired.
    6. Serve hot and enjoy!


  • Soba

    Soba is made of buckwheat noodles and is often served with dipping sauce or hot broth. It became popular during the Edo period because it was nutritious and easy to prepare. Soba was commonly eaten by working people. Today, soba is still widely eaten and is traditionally enjoyed on New Year's Eve for good luck.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 200g dried soba noodles
    - 4 cups dashi stock
    - 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    - 1 tablespoon mirin
    - Sliced green onions
    - Wasabi (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. Cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water. Set aside.
    2. In a pot, bring the dashi stock to a boil. Add soy sauce and mirin. Simmer for 5 minutes.
    3. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls.
    4. Pour the hot broth over the noodles or serve the broth on the side for dipping.
    5. Top with sliced green onions and a small amount of wasabi if desired.
    6. Serve and enjoy!


  • Bento

    Bento is made of rice, fish or meat, and side dishes packed into one box. It began as a simple meal for travelers, farmers, and workers. Over time, bento became more organized and decorative. Today, bento boxes are carefully prepared and sold in schools, stores, and train stations.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - Cooked rice
    - Grilled chicken or fish
    - Steamed vegetables (like broccoli, carrots)
    - Pickled vegetables (like pickled radish)
    - Tamagoyaki (Japanese rolled omelette)
    - Cherry tomatoes or fruit for garnish

    Instructions:
    1. Prepare all the components: cook the rice, grill the chicken or fish, steam the vegetables, and make tamagoyaki.
    2. In a bento box or container, start by placing a portion of cooked rice in one section.
    3. Arrange the grilled chicken or fish in another section.
    4. Add steamed vegetables and pickled vegetables in separate sections.
    5. Place slices of tamagoyaki in another section.
    6. Garnish with cherry tomatoes or fruit for added color and nutrition.
    7. Close the bento box and enjoy your meal!


  • Mochi

    Mochi is made of pounded glutinous rice formed into soft cakes. It has long been used in religious ceremonies and special celebrations. Mochi symbolizes good fortune and prosperity. Today, mochi is enjoyed as desserts, snacks, and seasonal sweets all year round.


    Recipie

    Ingredients:
    - 1 cup glutinous rice flour (mochiko)
    - 1 cup water
    - 1/4 cup sugar
    - Cornstarch or potato starch for dusting
    - Sweet red bean paste (anko) for filling (optional)

    Instructions:
    1. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour, water, and sugar until smooth.
    2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave on high for 2 minutes. Stir the mixture.
    3. Microwave for another 1-2 minutes until the mochi becomes thick and sticky.
    4. Dust a clean surface with cornstarch or potato starch. Transfer the mochi onto the surface and let it cool slightly.
    5. Divide the mochi into small portions. If using, flatten each portion and place a small amount of sweet red bean paste in the center. Fold the mochi around the filling and shape into a ball.
    6. Dust off any excess starch and serve.
